## Introduction
Today, I want to investigate yet another wild guess by famous German Youtuber Thomas Panke, aka [Held der Steine](https://www.youtube.com/@HeldderSteine).
Being an exclusive LEGO enthusiast and seller some years ago, he's now one of the most watched critics of the company.
Especially in the LEGO fan community, he's considered very rude, always pushing against LEGO while being overly positive in reviews for competitor's products, often neglecting critical points he highlights in products of the market leader.

In one of his [recent vieos](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ch2fkLxc7Yo) he takes a look on this year's [LEGO 10361 Holiday Express](https://www.lego.com/de-de/product/holiday-express-train-10361)*.
One of his points addresses the 3D printed mini-model of a train.
His claim is that this is the company's attempt to further reduce production costs: ["Es ist noch billiger in der Herstellung"](https://youtu.be/Ch2fkLxc7Yo?si=aJybA_D92mcvb_G_&t=769) ("It's even cheaper in production", translation by me).
One can only imagine that her refers to 3D printing is cheaper that the usual injection moulding, LEGO uses for producing most of their bricks.
